Fride Rice

30 minutes

4 servings

How to Make It

- If you're using freshly cooked rice, let it cool completely, or use leftover rice as it's less sticky and ideal for fried rice.
- In a large pan or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and scramble until fully cooked. Remove the eggs from the pan and set them aside.
- In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil. SautÃ© the garlic and onion for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and softened.
- Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until they are cooked but still crisp.
- Add the cold rice to the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry the rice with the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the rice to heat through and slightly crisp up.
- Add soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sesame oil, and white pepper. Stir everything together so that the rice is evenly coated with the sauce.
- Add the scrambled eggs back into the pan and mix them into the rice.
- Garnish with chopped green onions and serve immediately.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any oil of your choice)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, corn, or bell peppers)
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper (optional)
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste
